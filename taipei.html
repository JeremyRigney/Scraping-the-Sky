Taipei 101, formerly known as the Taipei World Financial Center, is a landmark supertall skyscraper in Xinyi District, Taipei, Taiwan. Construction began in June 1998 and the building opened on December 31, 2004
TAIPEI is mnemonic for Technology, Art, Innovation, People, Environment, and Identity. 101 represents the concept of striving for beyond perfection.
ARCHITECTURE
Address:No. 45, Shifu Road, Xinyi District, Taipei City, Taiwan 110
Owner: Taipei Financial Center Corporation
Architects: C.Y.Lee & Partners
Height: 509 meters
Design represents a bamboo stalk to symbolise Youth, Longevity and Everlasting Strength.
DID YOU KNOW?
As Taipei 101 sits just 200meters away from a major faultline, a 700 tonne steel pendulum is suspended from the 92nd to the 87th floor that serves as a tuned mass damper. The $4 million pendulum sways to offset movement in the building caused by strong gusts and, of course, earthquakes. It is estimated that Taipei 101 can sway up to 40% without collapsing.
