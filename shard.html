The Burj Khalifa is a skyscraper located in Dubai, United Arab Emirates. It is the tallest man-made structure in the world, standing at approximately 830 m.
During the peak of construction, atleast 12,000 workers worked on the construction site per day. Construction started on the 21st September, 2004 and was officially opened on the 4th of January, 2010.
The Burj Khalifa was built at a cost of $1.5 billion and has 163 floors.
ARCHITECTURE
Address: 1 Sheikh Mohammed bin Rashid Blvd - Dubai - United Arab Emirates
Owner: Emaar Properties
Architects: Adrian Smith, William F. Baker, Marshall Strabala, George J. Efstathiou
Height: 828 meters
The building design takes after the Greek flower, Hymenocallis.
