CONSTRUCTION
The Burj Khalifa is a skyscraper located in Dubai, United Arab Emirates. It is the tallest man-made structure in the world, standing at approximately 830m.
During the peak of construction, at least 12,000 workers were employed on the construction site daily. Construction started on the 21st September, 2004 and the skyscraper was officially opened on the 4th of January, 2010.
The Burj Khalifa was built at a cost of $1.5 billion and has 163 floors.
ARCHITECTURE
Address: 1 Sheikh Mohammed bin Rashid Blvd - Dubai - United Arab Emirates
Owner: Emaar Properties
Architects: Adrian Smith, William F. Baker, Marshall Strabala, George J. Efstathiou
Height: 828 meters
The building design takes after the Greek flower, Hymenocallis.
